# Chapter 2: Staredown

You have entered another dungeon. You are standing at the bottom of the staircase where you entered, facing east. In a haystack by the staircase, you find a needle. It's a pathfinder's pin. Concerned for the safety of any beasts of burden that might stray here to feed, you decide to keep the pin. You press it into your magic map. As you explore a dungeon, the pin moves with you on the map. The pin also tracks your position as a number, counted from the start of the map in left-to-right, top-to-bottom reading order, considering only symbols (not blank spaces). The top-left symbol is at position zero.

The top level is at the top of the map, and the bottom level is at the bottom of the map. On the top level, the only staircase leading up is the one where you entered.

There are many other staircases in this dungeon. Each staircase, except the one where you entered, has two ends: a top (marked `>`) and a bottom (marked `<`). The bottom is located directly below the top, on the next lower level. Whenever you walk onto either end of a staircase, you immediately follow it to the other end during the same tick. You end the tick standing at the other end of the staircase, facing the same direction as before.

You leave the dungeon by returning to the staircase where you entered. After how many ticks do you leave the dungeon?
